# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Women in the Mississauga–GTA area learning to drive for their G2 or G licence. Many are from South Asian backgrounds (English, Urdu, and Hindi spoken). They arrive primarily via Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp links — overwhelmingly on mobile. Some are first-time drivers nervous about committing; others are experienced drivers who've been postponing the G test. They need to feel safe and understood before handing money to a stranger.

## Product Purpose

Driving With Nayyer is a women-only driving instruction business run by Nayyer Sultana, MTO Certified Instructor, in Mississauga, Ontario. The website's job is to convert social media traffic into paid first-lesson bookings — eliminating the inquiry-and-back-and-forth that costs Nayyer unpaid time holds. Success means a visitor understands the offer, trusts the instructor, selects a package, and completes checkout through Cal.com + Stripe before closing the tab. No accounts, no custom backend — static marketing with external booking.

## Brand Personality

Professional, Confident, Empowering. The instructor is a credentialed expert. The service is not merely convenient — it gives women the independence of driving. The tone is direct and warm, never hustle-y or cold. Copy is plain and clear, accessible to readers whose first language is Urdu or Hindi.

## Anti-references

- Generic local driving school sites: clip-art steering wheels, garish yellow/orange/red safety color palettes, dated two-column tables, tiny cramped fonts
- Pastel Canva-template Instagram small-business aesthetic: soft pink, cursive script fonts, floral motifs, excessive roundedness
- Cold corporate institutional look: government-site typography, clinical blues and grays, zero personality
- Busy multi-column layouts that overwhelm a mobile visitor arriving from a social media link

## Design Principles

1. **Trust before transaction** — each section earns confidence (instructor credentials, real photos, testimonials, policies) before asking for money; the booking CTA never feels premature
2. **Mobile-first, one-finger scroll** — the experience begins on a phone opened from a WhatsApp link; every tap target, font size, and section pacing is designed for that context first
3. **Women-first without over-explaining** — the positioning is stated clearly and quickly, then the design moves on; it does not need to repeat or justify itself
4. **Content carries the page** — real photos of Nayyer, the car, and students do more work than decorative elements; imagery is the design
5. **Clear path, minimal friction** — a visitor should reach the right Cal.com booking flow in two taps from anywhere on the page

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Mobile-first priority — most traffic arrives from Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp on a phone. Text must be scannable for users whose primary language is Urdu or Hindi (short sentences, avoid heavy idiomatic English). Touch targets ≥ 44px. Every animation must have a prefers-reduced-motion alternative.
